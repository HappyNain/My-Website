M16 Rifle

Overview

The M16 is a lightweight, air-cooled, gas-operated, magazine-fed assault rifle that was designed by Eugene Stoner in the late 1950s. It has been the standard issue rifle for U.S. military forces since the Vietnam War.

Specifications

  • Caliber: 5.56x45mm NATO
  • Length: 1,000 mm (39.0 in)
  • Barrel Length: 508 mm (20.0 in)
  • Weight: 3.4 kg (7.5 lb) (unloaded)
  • Rate of Fire: 700-950 rounds/min
  • Effective Range: 550 meters (600 yards)
  • Feed System: 20 or 30-round detachable magazine

How to Reload the M16

  1. Ensure the weapon is pointed in a safe direction and the safety is on.
  2. Press the magazine release button to remove the empty magazine.
  3. Insert a fully loaded magazine into the magazine well until it clicks into place.
  4. Pull the charging handle back and release it to chamber a round.
  5. Switch the safety to the "fire" position.
