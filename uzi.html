Uzi Submachine Gun

Overview

The Uzi is a family of Israeli open-bolt submachine guns designed by Uziel Gal in the 1950s. Compact and easy to use, the Uzi has become iconic and is widely recognized for its distinctive design and high rate of fire, making it a popular choice among military and law enforcement agencies worldwide.

Specifications

  • Caliber: 9x19mm Parabellum
  • Length: 600 mm (23.6 in) (stock extended)
  • Barrel Length: 260 mm (10.2 in)
  • Weight: 3.5 kg (7.7 lb) (unloaded)
  • Rate of Fire: 600 rounds/min
  • Effective Range: 200 meters (218 yards)
  • Feed System: 25 or 32-round detachable magazines

How to Reload the Uzi

  1. Ensure the weapon is pointed in a safe direction and the safety is on.
  2. Press the magazine release button to remove the empty magazine.
  3. Insert a fully loaded magazine into the magazine well until it clicks into place.
  4. Pull the charging handle back to chamber a round.
  5. Switch the safety to the "fire" position.
